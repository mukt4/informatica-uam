Mitchell College

From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia

Jump to: navigation, search

For the North Carolina college formerly known as Mitchell College, see Mitchell Community College.

Mitchell College is a liberal arts college located on the banks of the Thames River, in New London, Connecticut, USA. It was founded in 1938 on the former estate of Alfred Mitchell; its campus offers such amenities as on-campus housing (including four multi-level, dormitory-style buildings and four residential river houses), a cafe, ice cream shop, preserve area known as 'Mitchell Woods', multiple playing fields, and a beach. Notable for its Criminal Justice, Early Childhood Education, Sports Management, and Business Administration programs, the school boasts NEASC accredited programs in a wide array of academic disciplines with such student life options as its top notch athletic programs in such sports as basketball, lacrosse, soccer, baseball, and softball (whose members compete as the Mariners in Division III of the NCAA).

Mitchell is officially a dry campus, with a professional public safety program.
